There are 100 prisoners in solitary cells, each with a unique number from 1 to 100. A room contains 100 boxes, each containing a unique number from 1 to 100. Each prisoner will be allowed to enter the room, open 50 boxes, and check the numbers inside. They must leave the room exactly as they found it and can't communicate with other prisoners. If all 100 prisoners find their own number, they will all be freed. What strategy can they use to maximize their chance of success?
Initial reasoning chain
Let's try to figure out this prisoner problem. So, we have 100 prisoners, each with a unique number from 1 to 100. There are 100 boxes in a room, each containing a unique number from 1 to 100. Each prisoner can open 50 boxes, and they can't communicate after that. They all need to find their own number to be freed. The question is, what strategy maximizes their chance of success?

The optimal strategy leverages permutation cycle properties to achieve a surprisingly high success rate of approximately 31%. Each prisoner begins by opening the box corresponding to their own number, then follows the cycle until they find their number or exhaust their 50 attempts.

Synthesis (post-thinking)
After analyzing various approaches, the cycle detection strategy clearly emerges as the optimal solution to this prisoner problem. The key insight is recognizing that the random arrangement of numbers in boxes forms a permutation, which can be decomposed into disjoint cycles.
